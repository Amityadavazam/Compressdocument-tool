1. Policy Overview

compress Document Pro Suite ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to complying with U.S. copyright law and the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This policy outlines our procedures for addressing copyright infringement claims.

Important: Our platform processes files locally in users' browsers. We do not store or host user files on our servers. However, we respond to valid DMCA notices regarding any allegedly infringing content that may be linked or referenced on our website.

1.1. Our Commitment

  • Respect intellectual property rights
  • Respond promptly to infringement notices
  • Cooperate with copyright owners
  • Maintain a repeat infringer policy

2. Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe that your copyrighted work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement and is accessible through our Service, you may notify our copyright agent as set forth in the DMCA.

2.1. Who Can File

  • The copyright owner
  • Authorized agent of the copyright owner
  • Legal representative with proper authorization

Warning: Filing a false claim may result in legal consequences including liability for damages under Section 512(f) of the DMCA.

2.2. What We Require

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed
  • Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing
  • Contact information of the complaining party
  • A statement of good faith belief
  • A statement under penalty of perjury

3. DMCA Takedown Notice Requirements

To be effective under the DMCA, your infringement notification must include the following information:

Legal Requirement: Your notice must comply with 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3) to be considered valid.

3.1. Required Information

  1. Physical or electronic signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed
  3. Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing
  4. Contact information sufficient to permit us to contact you
  5. Statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material is not authorized
  6. Statement that the information in the notification is accurate
  7. Statement made under penalty of perjury that you are authorized to act on behalf of the owner

3.2. Notice Validity

  • Must be in writing
  • Must contain all required elements
  • Must be sent to our designated agent
  • Must be specific and detailed

5. Takedown Process

Our process for handling DMCA takedown notices is as follows:

5.1. Receipt of Notice

  1. Notice received by designated agent
  2. Initial review for completeness and validity
  3. Notification of receipt sent to complainant
  4. Evaluation of the claim

5.2. Actions Taken

  • For Valid Notices:
    • Removal or disablement of access to infringing material
    • Notification to the user who posted the material
    • Record of the takedown in our system
  • For Invalid Notices:
    • Request for additional information
    • Explanation of deficiencies
    • Opportunity to correct

Timeline: We aim to process valid DMCA notices within 24-48 hours of receipt. Complex cases may require additional time.

6. Counter-Notification Process

If you believe your material was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification:

Right to Counter-Notify

Users have the right to file a counter-notification under 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3).

6.1. Counter-Notification Requirements

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the material that has been removed
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake
  4. Your name, address, and telephone number
  5. A statement consenting to the jurisdiction of your local Federal District Court

6.2. Counter-Notification Process

  • Submit counter-notification to our designated agent
  • We forward it to the original complainant
  • Material may be restored in 10-14 business days unless legal action is taken
  • We may provide your contact information to the complainant

7. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA and other applicable laws, we maintain a policy for terminating users who are repeat infringers.

Policy: Users who repeatedly infringe copyrights may have their accounts terminated and be banned from using our services.

7.1. Infringement Tracking

  • We track user infringement history
  • We consider all valid infringement claims
  • We review patterns of infringement

7.2. Termination Criteria

  • Multiple valid DMCA notices
  • Failure to respond to warnings
  • Deliberate infringement
  • Abuse of the service for infringement purposes
